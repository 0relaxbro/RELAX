How the math works: RELAX Score = 100 − average(FOMO, Panic). It's not just "100 minus FOMO" — a wallet with high FOMO but low Panic still lands somewhere in the middle, because RELAX Score reflects both signals together, not just one of them.

How this actually works

  • We fetch a wallet's on-chain activity from the last 30 days directly from Solana's public RPC — the same free, open network any wallet app uses. Very active wallets are capped at the most recent 60 transactions to keep results quick and consistent.
  • We look at swap frequency, how many different tokens were touched, and how tightly clustered the trades are in time.
  • Important: "Panic Signal" measures clustered, rapid-fire trading — not actual panic-selling, which would require matching trades against historical price data. We don't do that here. Think of it as "trading in bursts", not "sold at the bottom".
  • That gets turned into a simple, readable score — for fun and self-reflection, not financial advice.
  • We also estimate wallet age (how far back we can find activity) and average holding time (based on acquire → dispose pairs we spot within the same window). Both are estimates, not guarantees — a wallet with history beyond what we scan will show "60+ days" rather than an exact number.
  • No connection to the RELAX token contract, its authority, or its supply. This is a separate analysis layer, not a token feature.
  • The public RPC is shared by the whole world and rate-limited. If it's busy, the tool will say so instead of pretending to work.
